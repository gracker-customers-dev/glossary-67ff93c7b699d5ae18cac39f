Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)

April 17, 2025

PKI is a framework of policies, technologies, and procedures to create, manage, distribute, use, store, and revoke digital certificates and public keys for secure communications.

Description

Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) is a critical component in ensuring secure electronic transactions and communications, particularly in sectors like e-commerce and healthcare where data integrity and confidentiality are paramount. PKI uses a pair of cryptographic keys, a public key and a private key, to encrypt and decrypt data. The public key is shared openly, while the private key remains confidential to the owner. PKI enables authentication, data integrity, and non-repudiation through digital certificates issued by a Certificate Authority (CA). In e-commerce, PKI helps secure online transactions, ensuring that sensitive customer data, such as credit card information, is transmitted securely. In healthcare, PKI supports the secure sharing of patient information between providers, maintaining compliance with regulations like HIPAA by protecting against unauthorized access and ensuring data integrity. By leveraging PKI, organizations can build trust with users and partners by ensuring that communications and data exchanges are secure and verifiable.

Examples

  • Example 1: In e-commerce, PKI secures transactions by encrypting credit card information sent to online retailers, ensuring data confidentiality and integrity.
  • Example 2: In healthcare, PKI is used to encrypt patient records when they are transmitted between healthcare providers, protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access.

Additional Information

  • Additional info 1: PKI involves the use of digital certificates which are issued by trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs) to verify the identity of individuals or organizations.
  • Additional info 2: PKI supports various security services such as confidentiality, integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation, which are essential for maintaining secure communications and data exchanges.
