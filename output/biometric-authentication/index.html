Biometric Authentication

April 17, 2025

Biometric authentication is a security process that uses unique biological characteristics to verify an individual's identity.

Description

Biometric authentication involves using an individual's unique physical or behavioral traits to verify their identity. In the context of e-commerce and healthcare, this method enhances security and convenience by reducing reliance on traditional passwords, which can be compromised. Biometric systems capture and store the biometric data, such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris patterns, and compare it to the data provided during authentication attempts. In e-commerce, biometric authentication can streamline the purchase process, improve customer experience, and prevent fraudulent transactions. In healthcare, it ensures secure access to sensitive patient data and maintains privacy by ensuring that only authorized personnel can access confidential information. The adoption of biometric authentication is growing due to its ability to provide a high level of security and its convenience for users. However, it also raises concerns about privacy, data breaches, and the ethical use of biometric data.

Examples

  • Fingerprint scanning for unlocking smartphones.
  • Facial recognition for accessing healthcare records.

Additional Information

  • Biometric data is stored in a secure database and matched against the live scan for authentication.
  • Biometric authentication is considered more secure than traditional passwords but raises privacy concerns.
