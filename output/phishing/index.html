Phishing

April 17, 2025

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate entities to deceive individuals into providing sensitive information.

Description

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities, often through email or other digital communication channels, to steal sensitive information such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal identification details. In the context of e-commerce, phishing can lead to unauthorized access to customer accounts, financial losses, and damage to the business's reputation. For healthcare, phishing attacks can result in the exposure of protected health information (PHI), which can have severe privacy implications and regulatory consequences under laws like HIPAA. Attackers often create fake websites that closely resemble legitimate ones, tricking users into entering their information. These attacks can also come in the form of messages with malicious attachments or links that download malware onto the victim’s device. The consequences of phishing can be devastating, leading to identity theft, financial loss, and compromised data security. Organizations must implement strong security measures, employee training, and awareness programs to combat phishing threats effectively.

Examples

  • An email that appears to be from an online retailer asking the user to verify their account information by clicking on a fraudulent link.
  • A message from what seems to be a healthcare provider prompting a patient to update their health records via a fake website.

Additional Information

  • Phishing attacks are often the first step in larger cyber attack campaigns, such as installing malware or conducting ransomware attacks.
  • Organizations can employ multi-factor authentication (MFA) and email filtering technologies to reduce the risk of successful phishing attacks.
