User Consent

April 17, 2025

User Consent refers to the permission given by a user for their data to be collected, processed, and used by a service or application.

Description

User Consent is a critical component in both e-commerce and healthcare sectors, ensuring that user data is handled in a transparent and ethical manner. In e-commerce, user consent is typically obtained when a user agrees to the terms and conditions of a website or application, allowing the service to use their personal information for processing orders, personalizing experiences, and marketing purposes. In healthcare, user consent is even more crucial due to the sensitive nature of personal health information (PHI). Healthcare providers must obtain explicit consent from patients before collecting and sharing their health data, in compliance with regulations such as the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) in the United States. In both industries, obtaining informed and explicit consent is essential not only for legal compliance but also for building trust with users. Users must be informed about what data is being collected, for what purpose, and with whom it will be shared, and they should have the ability to revoke their consent at any time.

Examples

  • A user checking a box to agree to a website's privacy policy before making a purchase.
  • A patient signing a consent form allowing a healthcare provider to share their medical records with a specialist.

Additional Information

  • User consent can be explicit (user actively gives permission) or implicit (consent is assumed under certain conditions).
  • GDPR requires that consent be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous, with an easy mechanism to withdraw it.
